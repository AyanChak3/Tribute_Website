Sachin Tendulkar

Born 24 April, 1973

Master Blaster

Sachin Tendulkar, widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers of all time, is a former Indian cricketer who made an immense impact on the sport. Known as the "Master Blaster," he holds numerous records, including being the first player to score 100 international centuries and the highest run-scorer in both Test and One Day International cricket. Tendulkar's career spanned over two decades, from 1989 to 2013, during which he became a national hero and a symbol of cricketing excellence. His dedication, humility, and skill earned him respect worldwide, and he remains a legendary figure in Indian sports history.

Biographics

  • Full Name: Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar
  • Birth Date: April 24, 1973
  • Place of Birth: Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
  • Debut: Made his debut for India at the age of 16 in a Test match against Pakistan in 1989.
  • Retirement: Retired from international cricket in 2013 after a legendary career spanning 24 years.
  • Achievements: First player to score 100 international centuries and the only player to have scored more than 30,000 runs in international cricket.
  • Awards: Recipient of India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, in 2014, just after his retirement.
